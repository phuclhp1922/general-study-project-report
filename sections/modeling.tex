\section{Modeling}
\label{sec:modeling}
We incorporate the modeling of the original paper, which is for wildfire,
into our context of high-building fire. 
We extend the modeling to include smoke at the spreading layer, as well as 
another allocation aspect of rescue teams. 
Despite our effort, more advanced modeling incorporating more factors is still needed
to better simulate the real-world scenario.

\subsection{Model Assumptions}
We approach this spatial allocation problem as a constrained optimization problem, 
following the modeling of the original paper. 
We construct our overall cost function with three separate objective functions: 
the total population lost, the total property loss, and the total cost of unit deployment,
in order of importance. 
To reflect this, we adopt the lexicographic (sequential) optimization strategy, 
which minimizes the objective functions in order.
