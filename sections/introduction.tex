\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Fire safety within contemporary built environments faces escalating challenges due to the increasing architectural complexity of high-rise structures, high occupancy densities, and the prevalence of synthetic materials that accelerate fire spread \cite{Abdullahi2025}. Traditional fire safety protocols, often rooted in static risk assessments and manual heuristic-based decision-making, are increasingly insufficient for handling the high-stakes, "Black Swan" events of modern urban settings \cite{Kinkel2025, Eslamzadeh2022}. Consequently, the field is undergoing a paradigm shift toward Multi-Criteria Decision-Making (MCDM) and Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) as dominant scientific frameworks for structuring these intractable problems \cite{MDPI_MCDM_2025, GrandaChico2025}.

The strategic management of fire protection services (FPS) has historically focused on Resource Allocation in Fire Departments (RAFD), primarily addressing macro-level objectives such as the optimal spatial placement of fire stations and the management of heterogeneous vehicle fleets \cite{Eslamzadeh2022, Fleet_Optimization_2025}. While these models utilize advanced operations research techniques—including Integer Programming (IP) and Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA)—to minimize social costs, a significant "tactical gap" remains \cite{Galande2025}. Current models often treat the incident site as a single point, failing to account for the internal 3D logistics of a burning building \cite{Zhang2024}.

This research bridges the gap between strategic planning and tactical execution by introducing \textbf{MORAv2}. By re-engineering the Multicriteria Optimal Resource Allocation (MORA) framework—originally developed for large-scale wildfire suppression—for indoor 3D topologies, we provide a solver capable of active decision-making \cite{Orphanoudakis2025}. MORAv2 integrates real-time data from Internet of Things (IoT) sensors and Digital Twin monitoring systems to evaluate a dynamic ``smoke factor,'' ensuring that tactical deployment strategies prioritize both life-safety and responder survivability \cite{ReyesNorambuena2025, Smith2025}. This study validates the model through a simulation-optimization approach, positioning MORAv2 as a critical component of the next-generation smart city safety infrastructure.
