\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}

\subsection{Evolution of MCDM in Fire Science}
Multi-Criteria Decision-Making (MCDM) has emerged as the dominant paradigm for structuring complex fire emergency problems 
\cite{math13050731}, \cite{GrandaChico2025}. 
While the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) remains a foundational tool for risk ranking, 
recent literature emphasizes the need for objective weighting methods like CRITIC and Entropy to reduce expert bias 
\cite{Patel2025}, \cite{Singh2023} \cite{math13050731}. 
Advanced logic systems, including Spherical Fuzzy Sets (SFS) and Neutrosophic logic, 
are increasingly utilized to handle the ``indeterminacy'' and epistemic uncertainty inherent in high-pressure disaster operations 
\cite{SphericalFuzzy_2023}, \cite{Neutrosophic_PERT_2025}, \cite{Doe2024}, \cite{Lee2025}.

\subsection{Strategic Resource Allocation and Fleet Management}
Resource Allocation in Fire Departments (RAFD) is a high-stakes domain focused on minimizing social costs—specifically life and property loss 
\cite{Eslamzadeh2022}. 
Literature reviews identify over 400 variables influencing resource distribution, ranging from population density to temporal demand shifts 
\cite{Eslamzadeh2022}, \cite{Garcia2024}. 
Recent research focuses on the management of heterogeneous fleets—combining engines, ladders, 
and SUVs—utilizing Multi-Depot Vehicle Routing (MDHF-VRPTW-SPD) and Queueing Models (M/G/$\infty$) to improve Effective Response Force (ERF) 
assembly times \cite{Brown2025}, \cite{Taylor2023}, \cite{Zhou2025}, \cite{Davis2026}, \cite{Kim2025}.

\subsection{IoT, Digital Twins, and 3D Fire Monitoring}
The shift toward ``Smart Firefighting'' is enabled by the bidirectional flow of data between physical environments and Digital Twins 
\cite{Smith2025}. Integration of IoT sensor arrays (temperature, CO, smoke) allows for the calculation of the Improved Risk Index (IRI), 
providing dynamic hazard visualization in multi-story residential buildings \cite{Abdullahi2025}. 
Knowledge-graph-based approaches further enhance this by fusing textual reports with live sensor data to
predict fire propagation laws autonomously \cite{Smith2025}, \cite{NIST2026}.

\subsection{Optimization Algorithms for Evacuation Efficiency}
Tactical response relies on high-speed pathfinding algorithms capable of operating in degraded environments \cite{Chen2025}, \cite{Guo2025}. 
Hybrid models like the PTS algorithm (Pathfinder and Tunicate Swarm) and GreyGNN-MARL 
have demonstrated significant reductions in evacuation times, ranging from 7.7\% to 25\% \cite{Chen2025}, \cite{GrandaChico2025}. 
These models are increasingly tested against bio-inspired metaheuristics, such as NSGA-III, 
to optimize critical infrastructure reliability under fire stress \cite{Guo2025}.


\subsection{Human Behavior and the ``Smoke Factor''}
One of the most critical trends is the interdisciplinary assessment of human behavior in fires (HBiF) \cite{Kinkel2025}, \cite{Fisher2024}. 
Scoping reviews identify nearly 40 research gaps, specifically regarding how toxic and irritant smoke affects wayfinding and decision-making 
\cite{Kinkel2025}. MORAv2 incorporates these nuances by treating the ``smoke factor'' as a dynamic constraint, 
prioritizing nodes for evacuation based on real-time visibility and toxicological thresholds \cite{Hurst2026}.

\subsection{MILP and the MORA Framework}
Operations research in wildland fire suppression has pioneered the use of Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP) for real-time hazard adaptation 
\cite{GrandaChico2025}, \cite{Orphanoudakis2025}. The MORA toolkit utilize lexicographic optimization to 
strictly prioritize life-safety over property and operational costs \cite{Orphanoudakis2025}, \cite{Ontario_Fire_Group_2024}. 
MORAv2 bridges the gap between these large-scale wildfire models and indoor tactical reality by 
mapping wildfire suppression logic onto 3D structural as multi 2D grid \cite{Orphanoudakis2025}, \cite{Chen2025}.
